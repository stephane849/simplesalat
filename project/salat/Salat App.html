Salāt — a prayer-times app for the Mudita Kompakt

Designed to the MMD E Ink rules: pure grayscale, warm paper, high contrast, generous whitespace, and no animation that would ghost on the 4.3″ 800×480 display. Type-led, with English primary and Arabic secondary set in Amiri. Navigation lives in the hardware bezel — the capacitive back arrow, home circle, and menu below the screen are all wired.

This is the finalized direction — Arc of the Day. The home plots the day's prayers along the sun's path: the solid trail is time elapsed, the sun marks now, and the next prayer sits below with its countdown. It's live and clickable — use the hardware menu (☰) for the full Hijri calendar, Qibla, Settings, and Adhan, where you can upload your own call to prayer.

The app

Tap any prayer name on the arc to read its time and countdown below; the live sun stays at “now”, and Now returns you there. Everything is reachable in-app via the menu (☰, top-right) — Calendar, Qibla, Settings, and Adhan (with custom-adhan upload) — since the Kompakt's hardware keys are OS-level.

Where I'd take it next

  1. Spelled-out times. The Arc plots every prayer; if you also want each time listed numerically, the Arc + Times layout is a drop-in (kept alongside as “Salāt App — Directions”).
  2. Real time calculation. I used fixed, realistic sample times for Ottawa on 8 Jun 2026. Production would compute from GPS + the chosen method, including the high-latitude rule that matters for an Ottawa summer.
  3. Adhan audio & MuditaOS hooks. Custom-adhan upload & preview is already wired in the prototype — production would bind the chosen sound and per-prayer toggles to the OS notification + sound layer.
  4. Localized RTL polish. Tune the Arabic across MuditaOS's 8 languages and refine an Arabic-primary layout option.
  5. Live Qibla. Bind the dial to the magnetometer; show a clear "aligned" state for E Ink rather than continuous rotation.
